Role: First-time customer, health-conscious, moderate tech literacy, ordering on mobile during a 20-minute lunch break.
BLOCK A — FIRST CONTACT & MENTAL MODEL (5 min)
A1. The 5-Second Test
Land on the homepage with fresh eyes. Cover the rest of the page. In 5 seconds, answer:

What does this app sell?
Who is it for?
Why should I trust it over Swiggy?
Now scroll. Did the page confirm or contradict your first impression? Note the exact element that either built or broke trust first.

A2. Value Proposition Stress Test
"No Maida · No Refined Sugar · High Protein" — these claims appear in the hero. Now navigate the menu.

Pick any 3 random items. Do their names, descriptions, or tags substantiate those claims?
If you were a diabetic customer, could you confirm any item is safe based only on what the app shows? What's missing — macros table? Glycaemic index? Allergen list?
Does "For Healthy Soul" feel earned by the time you've browsed 2 minutes, or does it feel like a tagline the food doesn't back up?
A3. Competitive Context
You just closed Swiggy, where you saw 200+ restaurants. Why would you stay here?

Is there a clear answer visible without scrolling?
Does the app give you a reason to skip restaurant variety for a single-brand experience?
BLOCK B — MENU EXPLORATION (10 min)
B1. Diet Confidence Test
You are a strict vegetarian. Navigate the full menu.

Is it possible to accidentally add a non-veg item without noticing?
The green/red dot system — does it hold up under speed? Tap Veg filter, then scroll fast. Do any ambiguous items appear?
Now switch to Non-Veg. Does the filter update instantly and completely?
B2. Decision Fatigue Map
Navigate every category in order (Healthy Meals → Desserts).

At which category do you stop reading descriptions carefully?
Which category has the weakest item descriptions?
After 8 categories, can you remember what distinguished Healthy Meals from Meal Boxes?
Is there a natural "this is enough, I'll order now" moment, or does the app exhaust you?
B3. The Search Trap
Type "chicken" in the search bar.

How many results appear? Are they ordered by relevance or arbitrarily?
Type "tikka" — do you get both the wrap and the bowl?
Type "protien" (misspelled) — what happens?
Clear the search and switch to a category. Does the diet filter state persist, or reset?
B4. Price Anchoring Check
Without looking at your cart total, estimate what you'd spend on a satisfying lunch (wrap + drink).

Now actually add those items. Was your mental estimate within ₹50?
Is there a price-to-value signal on the menu (e.g., calorie count, portion size) that would have helped you estimate?
The ₹150 Quinoa Khichdi vs ₹359 Chicken Tikka Wrap — without descriptions, would you assume quality difference or portion difference?
BLOCK C — DISH DETAIL & CUSTOMIZATION (10 min)
C1. Customization Completeness Test
Open Chicken Tikka Wrap. Make these changes:

Switch sauce to Peri Peri
Upgrade to Multigrain Wrap
Toggle Subscribe & Save on
Change quantity to 3
Now look at the sticky bottom bar price. Does it accurately reflect: base + ₹10 upgrade + 15% discount × 3?

If the math is wrong, note exactly which step broke it.
What happens if you toggle Subscribe off and back on — does the price restore correctly?
C2. Subscription Trust Test
"Subscribe & Save 15%" — before toggling it:

Do you understand what you're committing to?
Is the delivery frequency stated anywhere visible without toggling?
Is there a cancellation promise before you commit?
Toggle it on, add to cart, go to checkout. Where does the subscription show up? Can you cancel from checkout?
C3. Pairing Intelligence Test
Open any dish. Look at "Pairs well with."

Are the pairings nutritionally logical (high protein meal + protein smoothie is redundant)?
Click a pairing item. Does the back navigation work correctly and land you back where you'd expect?
If you add both the main dish and its pairing to cart, does the cart total calculate correctly?
C4. The Empty State
Navigate to a category with a non-veg filter active, then find a category that is 100% non-veg items and switch to Veg filter.

Does the app tell you there are no results, or does it just show a blank list?
Is there a recovery action (e.g., "Clear filter" or "Show all") offered?
BLOCK D — CART & CHECKOUT STRESS TEST (10 min)
D1. Cart Integrity Test
Add 5 different items across 3 categories. Then:

Increase item 2 to quantity 4
Remove item 4
Add item 4 back from the cart page (you can't — can you? What does the app let you do?)
Navigate away to the homepage and come back
Are all quantities and items exactly as you left them? Does the subtotal and delivery fee update live?

D2. Free Delivery Threshold Manipulation
Add items totalling ₹440 (under the ₹499 free delivery threshold).

Is the gap (₹59 to free delivery) displayed prominently?
Does the app suggest specific items that would close the gap?
Now add an item that brings you to exactly ₹499. Does the delivery fee drop to zero instantly?
Remove that item. Does the ₹49 fee return?
D3. Checkout Abandonment Points
Go through checkout step by step and note every moment where you feel uncertain enough to quit:

Is there a point where you don't trust the address selection?
Does the rider tip feel pressured or optional?
Is "Razorpay Secure Checkout" enough to make you comfortable entering payment info?
At what exact screen would a first-time user most likely close the app?
D4. The Phone Number Gap
At address selection — none of the addresses list your phone number visibly. How does the rider contact you? Is that explained anywhere before you pay?

BLOCK E — POST-ORDER & SUPPORT (5 min)
E1. Track Page Disorientation
You just paid. The Track page shows order #TAN-202605-8843.

Is this the order you just placed? How do you know?
The ETA shows 25 min. What time did you place the order? Is the absolute delivery time shown anywhere?
Click "Next Status →" twice. Does the rider card appear at the right moment? Is it the right moment?
What happens if you refresh the page — does the simulated status persist or reset?
E2. Support Agent Trust Test
Open the chat widget and ask:

"I just placed order #8843 — can you change the sauce to BBQ?"
Wait for the reply. Is it accurate, helpful, or a brush-off?
Ask: "I'm allergic to peanuts. Is the Peri Peri sauce safe?"
Ask: "I want to cancel my order immediately."
Does the agent ever tell you something that could be wrong but sounds confident? That's a critical failure mode.

E3. Orders Page Completeness
Navigate to Orders (header nav).

Can you reorder your last meal in two taps?
Is there a way to see what customizations were applied to past orders?
If you want to dispute an order — wrong item delivered — what do you do from this page?
